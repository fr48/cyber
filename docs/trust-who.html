Trust After a Breach

People forgive companies for data breaches pretty quickly. While there is an initial drop in the company's stock price after a breach, prices in general rebound back to previous levels.

Fahmida Y Rashid

Published December 14, 2019

For many people, a data breach is good enough reason to avoid that business for a while. Perhaps they worry about the way the organization handled user information, or how the attackers got in to the systems. In the Pew Research suvey, 70 percent of the respondents said they have refused to set up accounts at websites over security and privacy concerns. However, a look at company stock prices suggest that consumers forgive companies for security incidents and data breaches pretty quickly. The NASDAQ has been pretty steady over the past two years, so the fluctuations are not part of the market's natural downturns.
